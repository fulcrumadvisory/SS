# Savor & Simplify Business and Marketing Plan

## Vision

Savor & Simplify is a brand for practical solutions that help households, families, small businesses, and non-profits simplify real life.

Most people are surrounded by disconnected apps. One app handles one part of a wedding. Another tracks a home device. Another manages a list. Another sends alerts. The customer becomes the integration layer.

Savor & Simplify exists to simplify that burden. The brand can support multiple offerings, including apps, templates, dashboards, monitoring, concierge setup, advisory services, and AI-assisted workflows.

## Five-Year Goal

Build recurring revenue under Savor and Simplify Marketing LLC while the founders continue full-time day jobs, creating a practical path toward retirement within five years.

## Positioning

Savor & Simplify is the brand umbrella. The life-operations app is one possible offering under that brand, not the whole company.

Plain-language promise:

> Practical solutions for real life, built to reduce friction without adding another system to babysit.

## Target Customers

### Households

Families managing homes, events, repairs, purchases, subscriptions, seasonal tasks, and aging-parent or child-related responsibilities.

### Life Event Customers

People planning weddings, reunions, graduations, milestone birthdays, memorials, fundraisers, or major family transitions.

### Small Businesses

Owner-operated businesses that need lightweight coordination, purchasing intelligence, reminders, vendor tracking, and simple operations support.

### Non-Profits

Churches, civic groups, school groups, clubs, and small non-profits that manage events, volunteers, budgets, vendors, and recurring activities.

### Home and Property Owners

Homeowners, rental-property owners, and pool owners who want proactive monitoring, alerts, and support without becoming automation experts.

### Relationship-Focused Customers

People who want help maintaining friendships, family ties, professional relationships, donor relationships, volunteer relationships, and other important personal or community connections.

## Offering Pillars

### 1. Life Event Hub

Possible app or service for weddings, reunions, fundraisers, corporate events, graduations, and other life events.

Core value:

- Tasks, budget, vendors, guests, documents, payments, timelines, and AI planning guidance in one place.

### 2. Home Automation

Dashboard, setup, and alerting offerings for smart homes and high-maintenance properties.

Core value:

- Home health visibility, humidity and leak monitoring, energy awareness, device status, and proactive alerts.

### 3. Pool Monitoring

Optional add-on for homes with pools.

Core value:

- pH, ORP, temperature, pump status, heater status, and maintenance alerts.

### 4. Price Watcher

Generic price and availability tracking agent that can stand alone or support other offerings.

Core value:

- Watch products, supplies, seasonal inventory, event materials, home equipment, pool supplies, and other customer-defined items.

### 5. Personal CRM

Relationship support for friends, family, customers, donors, volunteers, and professional contacts.

Core value:

- Remember birthdays, anniversaries, milestones, last-contact dates, key talking points, promises made, follow-up timing, and thoughtful reasons to reach out.
- Generate automated touchpoint reminders without making relationships feel transactional.
- Help households, small businesses, and non-profits maintain meaningful connection over time.

### 6. Concierge Setup

High-touch setup and support for customers who want help configuring offerings, devices, lists, and workflows.

Core value:

- Turn messy real life into usable solutions without forcing customers to do all setup themselves.

## Revenue Model

### Recurring Subscriptions

| Tier | Concept | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Starter | One offering, light usage | Good entry point |
| Household | Selected home, event, and task support | Core consumer tier |
| Home + Pool | Home dashboard plus pool monitoring | Higher-value property tier |
| Event Pro | Life Event Hub for planners, businesses, or non-profits | Higher workflow depth |
| Watcher | Product and deal tracking | Per-watchlist or category-based |
| Relationship | Personal CRM touchpoints, reminders, and key talking points | Families, professionals, small businesses, non-profits |
| Concierge | Setup help and premium support | Setup fee plus monthly support |

### Additional Revenue

- Setup fees
- Custom dashboard setup
- Device onboarding
- Event templates
- Personal CRM imports and relationship setup
- Vendor referral revenue
- Premium alerts
- Sponsored vendor placements, later and only if trust is protected

## Go-To-Market Strategy

### Phase 1: Foundation

- Launch the public website.
- Explain the problem clearly.
- Present Savor & Simplify as the brand umbrella and the first offerings as practical solutions.
- Use Pettigrew and Paradise as internal pilots.
- Use Life Event Hub as the first planning product demonstration.

### Phase 2: Pilot Customers

- Start with trusted local households, family contacts, small businesses, and non-profits.
- Offer concierge onboarding.
- Manually support workflows before automating everything.
- Track which offerings create the strongest willingness to pay.

### Phase 3: Repeatable Packages

- Turn repeated customer needs into packaged offerings and subscriptions.
- Standardize onboarding checklists.
- Build template libraries.
- Add payments and subscriber accounts.
- Decide which offerings stay self-service and which remain concierge-led.

### Phase 4: Scalable Offering Platform

- Add secure subscriber dashboards where the offering requires them.
- Add AI assistant workflows.
- Add background monitoring jobs.
- Move dynamic app services to Azure when needed.
- Keep public brand and marketing pages lightweight and fast.

## Hosting Recommendation

Start with GitHub Pages for the public website because it is simple, free, fast, and enough for a static marketing site.

Use Azure when the product needs:

- Subscriber login
- Payments
- Private dashboards
- Databases
- Background jobs
- API integrations
- AI workflow orchestration
- Secure device or event data

Recommended path:

1. GitHub Pages for the marketing website now.
2. Azure Static Web Apps or Azure App Service for subscriber-facing app offerings later.
3. Azure Functions for background jobs, alerts, price checks, and integrations.
4. Azure SQL, PostgreSQL, or Cosmos DB when persistent customer data is required.
