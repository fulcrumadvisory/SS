# Savor & Simplify Startup and Investor Plan

## Core Thesis

Savor & Simplify is a brand for practical solutions that help people manage important life moments, relationships, tasks, memories, and continuity across time.

The strongest first wedge is events because events create urgency, budgets, deadlines, vendors, relationships, documents, decisions, and memories. The long-term retention layer is relationship management and continuity: reminders, key dates, family knowledge, media, notes, and lessons learned that carry forward after the event ends.

## Five-Year Financial Objective

The business must be evaluated against a serious income-replacement goal: supporting two professionals retiring from approximately `$500,000/year` in full-time job income.

This means the goal is not simply "make a useful app." The goal is to build a recurring-revenue business that can support owner income, taxes, operating expenses, software costs, customer support, marketing, and reinvestment.

Conclusion: a low-cost consumer-only app is risky unless distribution becomes very strong. The business likely needs a mix of subscriptions, higher-value event packages, concierge setup, templates, and organizational tiers.

- contracts

## Initial Beachhead Markets

Do not launch to "everyone." Start with focused markets where urgency and willingness to pay are higher.

### Best early candidates

1. Weddings and wedding-adjacent family planning
2. Family reunions
3. Graduation and milestone celebrations
4. Memorials / celebration-of-life planning
5. Non-profit fundraisers and galas
6. Church / school / civic group events

### Strongest near-term wedge

Non-profit and community events may be stronger than pure consumer weddings because:

- repeat events happen annually
- organizers change and need continuity
- donor/volunteer relationship history matters
- budgets and vendors repeat
- the organization can justify paying
- concierge setup is easier to sell

## Pricing Strategy

Pricing must support serious revenue. Avoid only low-ticket consumer plans.

### Suggested early pricing

| Plan | Price | Target |
|---|---:|---|
| Free Preview | `$0` | One sample event, limited people/tasks |
| Personal | `$12/mo` or `$99/year` | Personal CRM and simple moments |
| Family/Event | `$29/mo` or `$249/year` | Active family events, reminders, memory vault |
| Event Pro | `$79/mo` | Repeat events, vendors, templates, multiple organizers |
| Organization | `$149-$299/mo` | Non-profits, schools, churches, civic groups |
| Concierge Setup | `$299-$1,500 one-time` | Import, setup, event buildout, training |

### Revenue path examples

Lean replacement path:

- 300 organizations at `$149/mo` = `$44,700 MRR`
- 500 families at `$29/mo` = `$14,500 MRR`
- 20 concierge setups/month at `$499` = `$9,980/mo`
- Total = about `$69,180/mo` or `$830k/year`

More consumer-heavy path:

- 2,000 families at `$29/mo` = `$58,000 MRR`
- 400 Personal users at `$12/mo` = `$4,800 MRR`
- 10 concierge setups/month at `$499` = `$4,990/mo`
- Total = about `$67,790/mo` or `$813k/year`

Investor-grade target:

- Aim for `$50k MRR` as the first retirement-relevant milestone.
- Aim for `$100k MRR` as the real five-year durability milestone.

## Go-To-Market Plan

### Phase 1: Manual Concierge Validation

Goal: prove people pay before building too much.

Actions:

- Interview 25 families / event organizers / non-profit leaders.
- Offer 5 concierge event setup pilots.
- Charge real money, even if discounted.
- Build manually with lightweight tools if needed.
- Track repeated pain points.

Success criteria:

- 5 paid pilots
- at least 3 customers willing to renew or expand
- clear repeatable onboarding checklist
- clear top 10 features customers actually used

### Phase 2: Productized Event Workspace

Goal: turn repeated pilot work into software.

Actions:

- Build Moments, People, Tasks, Vendors, Memory Vault.
- Add import/export.
- Add reminder workflows.
- Add AI-generated checklists and follow-ups.
- Create 3 templates: wedding, reunion, fundraiser.

Success criteria:

- 25 paying customers
- 10 active weekly users
- 5 repeatable templates
- churn reasons documented

### Phase 3: Organizational Tier

Goal: higher ARPA and recurring events.

Actions:

- Sell to churches, schools, civic groups, non-profits.
- Add multi-user roles.
- Add yearly event reuse.
- Add donor/volunteer relationship notes.
- Add board/staff handoff continuity.

Success criteria:

- 20 organizations paying `$149+/mo`
- repeat annual event usage
- at least 3 case studies

### Phase 4: Scale Content and Referrals

Goal: lower acquisition cost.

Actions:

- Publish planning templates.
- Build SEO around event checklists and family planning.
- Partner with planners, venues, photographers, churches, schools, and non-profits.
- Offer affiliate/referral program.

Success criteria:

- inbound leads
- repeatable CAC
- conversion by segment known

## Failure Modes

### 1. Too broad

Trying to build every offering at once will kill focus.

Mitigation:

- Start with Life Event Hub + Personal CRM.
- Keep home automation, pool monitoring, price watcher, and legacy as roadmap/offering pillars.

### 2. Too consumer-low-price

Consumer subscription churn can be brutal.

Mitigation:

- Include organization tiers.
- Sell concierge setup.
- Target repeat annual events.

### 3. Too much custom service

Concierge work can become consulting.

Mitigation:

- Turn every repeated concierge action into a template, import, checklist, or automation.

### 4. AI novelty trap

AI alone is not a moat.

Mitigation:

- Build around structured data: people, moments, tasks, vendors, notes, memories.

### 5. Trust barrier

Customers may hesitate to store family, relationship, or legacy data.

Mitigation:

- Clear privacy policy.
- Export anytime.
- Human language.
- No creepy automation.
- Reminders should feel thoughtful, not spammy.
